StoryAI

Project Overview

Client

The client is a large institution within the education sector, specializing in children’s literature and education.

Background

The client engaged our team to explore how generative AI could be leveraged to create a digital reading experience for their users. As digital products and services continue to grow rapidly, they saw a need to adapt their traditional content to new formats. The goal was to find meaningful ways to combine storytelling and learning with modern digital tools, while keeping the experience engaging and accessible for children.

The Solution

A mobile application for generating personalized children’s stories with interactive moments

Through research and iterative design, the team developed the concept of an interactive mobile application that lets parents create customized stories for their children using generative AI. The vision was to give parents quick and easy access to engaging stories tailored to their child’s interests, and the solution was designed to include:

  • Automatically generated, personalized children’s stories based on parent input — including character names, plot, and environment

  • A library to save and revisit favorite stories

  • Age-adapted content to suit a range of children

  • Interactive choice moments throughout each story, allowing the child to decide how the story continues in real time

Project Summary

Our team designed and developed a fully functional mobile application based on the concept above. Working in an agile workflow with iterative sprints, we moved from early research and prototyping through to a working product.

The application was built using React Native and JavaScript for the front-end, Firebase for data storage, Express.js to handle API requests, and Azure Web Apps to host the API.

The final product allows parents to generate a personalized children’s story by entering the character’s name, story environment, and plot — along with the child’s age to ensure age-appropriate content. Each story includes interactive choice moments where the child can decide how the narrative unfolds, for example: "Should Lisa open the red door or the green door?" The story then continues in real time based on that choice.

The solution demonstrated a significant improvement in children’s attention and engagement compared to reading a traditional book.

Project Approach

Process & Planning

Workflow

Agile workflow with iterative sprints

We tackled the project in agile, iterative sprints, which let us regularly check in on our progress, share what we were learning, and tweak our approach as new ideas came up. We also had frequent sync meetings to align on priorities, brainstorm solutions together, and plan our next steps.

Team Roles and Collaboration

Cross-functional team with defined areas of responsibility

Our team was a cross-functional group of four, with each of us taking the lead on one or two specific areas. This setup helped us stay organized and made it easy to collaborate closely across different skill sets.

My focus was on UX and UI design, where I handled everything from research and prototyping to user testing.

Design

Research

Market research and interviews with parents uncovered key challenges

I led the research phase, mixing market research with in-depth interviews with care givers. One major takeaway was that kids often have trouble staying focused during traditional reading sessions—a challenge that really shaped our design direction. Because of these insights, we decided to make interactivity and personalization a core part of the experience.

Ideate

Collaborative Workshop to Generate and Shape Ideas

To kick off the ideation phase, I organized a collaborative workshop with the team where we explored different ways to create a more engaging digital reading experience. We brainstormed a range of possibilities—everything from interactive features to new ways of personalizing stories for each child.

During the workshop, the core idea really took shape: a digital story app that let kids influence how the story unfolds through interactive choices, with content personalized to their interests, age, and even their name. This concept directly addressed the attention challenges uncovered in our research and became the foundation for our prototype and final solution.

Early Sketches

Mapping out the interaction and flow of the application

Prototypes

An iterative design process — from rough sketches to detailed wireframes

With the core concept defined, I moved into prototyping — starting with rough sketches to quickly explore layout ideas and user flows, then progressively refining them into detailed wireframes in Figma. Each iteration focused on keeping the experience simple and intuitive for parents, while making the story interaction feel natural and engaging for children.

User Testing

Design concepts were validated through A/B testing during the prototyping phase

To evaluate our design decisions, I conducted A/B testing with parents during the prototyping phase. Two variations of key screens were tested to compare how different layouts and interaction patterns affected usability and clarity. The feedback gathered directly shaped the final design — particularly around the story customization flow and how interactive choice moments were presented to the child.

Implementation

Frontend

React Native and JavaScript

Following research on frameworks and AI integration, the team built the application using React Native with JavaScript — a choice that offered cross-platform compatibility and straightforward integration with external services and APIs.

Data Storage

Firebase

We used Firebase to store our data because it provides a good way to handle real-time data updates, which we needed for the interactive parts where the story is updated with new content after each reader choice.

Backend

Express.js to handle API requests

We used Express.js to handle API requests and create a secure server environment. Express.js is simple, flexible, and fast, which made it a good fit for our project. It allowed us to build a POST API endpoint that works as a middle layer between our client app and OpenAI’s API. This setup helped us keep sensitive information, like API keys, safe on the server. We also used Firebase authentication to make sure only authorized users could access the API.

Azure Web App to host API

Our API was hosted with Azure Web App, which made it easy to deploy and manage in the cloud. Azure’s integration with GitHub allowed automatic updates every time we pushed new code, creating a smooth CI/CD workflow. This saved time and made it easier to focus on improving the application instead of handling manual deployments.

Result

Delivered solution

The result is a mobile application where parents can quickly create a personalized story by defining the character’s name, environment, and plot — along with the child’s age to ensure age-appropriate content. The application uses OpenAI’s API to generate each story dynamically based on the provided inputs.

Each story includes interactive choice moments where the child decides how the narrative continues — for example, "Should Lisa open the red door or the green door?" The story then unfolds in real time based on that choice. This interactivity was a direct response to the research insight around children’s limited attention during reading, and it proved to significantly improve engagement compared to traditional books.

Story categories:

  • Quick story: generates a story immediately, with no input required from the parent.

  • Custom story: a fully personalized story based on the parent’s instructions.

  • Emotion detective: follows the same format as a custom story, but ends with a reflective question about an emotion experienced during the narrative.

  • Vocabulary builder: a story designed to introduce and explore more advanced words, with in-context definitions and a vocabulary question at the end.
