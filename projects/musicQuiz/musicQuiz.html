MusicQuiz

A design concept for a music quiz feature that allows users to create, host, and participate in interactive and personalized quizzes.

Project Overview

About the project

Designing a music quiz feature for a music streaming app

As a project through out a course withe the focus on Design-Build-Test, me and my team of five students, designed a function for an mobile application in collaboration with a music streaming service in Sweden. The purpose of the function was to enable the users to create, host and join interactive music quizzes.

The main challenge was to create a clear information flow and ensuring smooth, parallel interaction between the host and the participants. To overcome this, we focused on an iterative design approach and conducted extensive user testing. The project resulted in an interactive Figma prototype that was presented to the company and well received.

Project Approach

Background

The background to our idea

A team of five who love to quiz together

The task was to find a company and develop a design-based solution for them, with a focus on the design–build–test process. Everyone on the team enjoys music quizzes, and it was something we often did together. This inspired the idea of designing a music quiz feature for a music streaming service. We then contacted a company within the music streaming industry, pitched our idea, and the project began.

The final idea

Interactive music quiz

The idea was to design a feature for their existing application that would enable users to create, host, and participate in music quizzes, either with their friends or with random people. Many online quizzes today only allow participants to answer the current question, but we wanted to make the quiz similar to a traditional music quiz with paper and pen, allowing participants to view the entire quiz and edit past questions.

We want the quiz creator to have the ability to generate questions with AI based on the participants' music tastes. This feature can help the creator quickly craft a quiz that aligns with the participants' preferences.

Target group

The target group for this project included people aged 20 to 40 with an interest in music quizzes.

Research

What do the users want?

Collected user insights in the context of traditional music quizzes on paper

To begin the project, we conducted a quantitative study to explore the target group’s needs, preferences, and motivations in the context of music quizzes. To gather this data, we created a Google Form with questions divided into different categories.

From prior research about music quizzes and from our own experience and assumptions, the target group has most experience with non-digital music quizzes, and therefore we formulated the form so that it did not reveal that our aim was to create a digital music quiz. Instead, we wanted to find user insights on traditional music quizzes in order to meet their needs and motivations in our design, solve their problems, and create something valuable for them with the help of technology.

Design-Build-Test

Design process

We conducted multiple design and test iterations.

Our design process went through many iterations as we discovered new ideas while testing prototypes with users. The tests we conducted between design iterations mainly included A/B testing and Think-Aloud testing. While designing, we followed the company’s graphic design guidelines and rules, which was both interesting and enjoyable. This allowed us to focus more on optimizing the user flow rather than on making design component choices.

One significant challenge was designing the UI while the game was ongoing. We had to create two interfaces for the same parts of the game: one view for the participants and another for the game host. When designing these two interfaces, it was crucial to consider how actions in one interface affected the other to ensure that the flow of events followed a logical order.

Result

Create quiz

This flow enables the user to create a music quiz from scratch with the help of templates for different types of questions or by using a generative AI function to get some questions delivered without any effort.

1. Create a new quiz.

2. Name the quiz.

3. Empty quiz – now you can add questions.

4. Add a question and choose between different question templates.

5. Add a song for the question.

6. Set the interval for the part you want to play for this question.

7. Based on the question template and chosen song, the answers for the question are generated. They can also be edited if needed.

8. The first question is now added.

9. To create questions with the AI generator, press the marked button.

10. Multiple questions are now generated. The songs in these questions are based on the participants’ playlists.

Host quiz

This flow demonstrates the interaction from the host’s view when it is ready to play the quiz.

1. Invite friends and start the quiz.

2. The quiz has started, but no questions are currently active. You can play the questions in any order. When a question is played, its answer form becomes available to all participants.

3. Start Question 1. Click on the question or drag the bar below to view more details.

4. View detailed information about the question. Use the number carousel below to navigate between questions. The active question is highlighted in green.

5. This is what it looks like when you navigate to a question that is not currently being played for the participants.

6. Now you are playing Question 2. This question includes four songs. You can either press the play button to play them in order, or select specific songs if you do not want to play them all.

7. When finished, you can review and correct the participants’ answers.

8. Overview of all the questions in the quiz.

9. Open to view the participants’ answers. The app automatically corrects them, but you can manually adjust the points if needed.

10. When you are done, you can present the results to the participants.

11. The winners are then displayed.

Participate in quiz

This is the interaction flow from the participants’ view.

1. Join the quiz via QR code or link.

2. Wait for the host to start the quiz.

3. The quiz has started and Question 1 is now playing.

4. Click on the question to open the answer form. The active question is highlighted in green.

5. Type your answer. You can always go back and edit it later.

6. Move to the next question and wait for it to become active.

7. If you are unsure about an answer but want to leave a note for later, you can add it in the highlighted box.

8. If you have added a note to a question, it will be indicated by the notebook icon.

9. Once the quiz is finished, the winners will be displayed.

10. Then you can take a closer look at your result and, if needed, appeal it to the host.
